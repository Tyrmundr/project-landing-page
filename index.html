The way of the warrior

Japanese culture is rich with traditions rooted in feudal times, historical moments full of honour and sacrifice. On this site you will be given a chance at learning more about mainly the history of Japan, but also a little about on of Japan's infamous elements: Bushidō. Other than bushido, Japan is famous for it's cuisine, it's beautiful natural landscapes, it's immense and sometimes silly folklore. Among other's you are going to be introduced to the kami, some versions of yōkai with great emphasis on the ambivalence and non-absolute nature of many such creatures.

A few points of interest

The beautiful cherry blossoms of Japan

Famed samurai melee weapon: the katana

Torii: a gate found in, or at the entrance of Shinto shrines

The fox, a messenger of the kami, Inari

If a man does not investigate into the matter of bushidō daily, it will be difficult for him to die a brave and manly death. Thus, it is essential to engrave this business of the warrior into one's mind well.

— Katō Kiyomasa
